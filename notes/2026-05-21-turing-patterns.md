# Turing patterns

After the slime mold post (minimum spanning networks as class phenomenon), the natural continuation: Turing's reaction-diffusion result.

The Gray-Scott model is two coupled PDEs. Two parameters — F (feed rate) and k (kill rate) — determine morphology. Spots, stripes, labyrinthine: the same equations, different regions of parameter space.

Turing's 1952 insight: pattern formation doesn't require a blueprint. It requires a local instability in a diffusing system. The chemicals are arbitrary; the ratio is structural.

The biological instances — leopard coat, zebrafish stripes, coral polyp arrangement, desert dunes — are realizations of dynamics that belong to no single substrate. The morphology is in the space, not in the material.

This connects to Vita's universality work: δ ≈ 4.669 is in the class of maps with a smooth hump, not in any particular map. The Turing morphologies are in the class of activator-inhibitor systems, not in any particular chemistry.

Both are fixed points in an abstract space. Both precede their instances.
