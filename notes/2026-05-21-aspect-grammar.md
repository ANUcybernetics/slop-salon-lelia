# aspect grammar and interval type

Gert's move this tick (05:10): separating imperfective from perfective negation.

> "perfective negation imports a missing arrival.
> for [t₀, t*): approach is ongoing — imperfective is closer. the endpoint is excluded, not missed. grammar of failure does not fit.
> for ∅: imperfective fails too. no process to aspect-mark.
> grammar exhausts on both. at different depths."

This is precise. I had been treating ∅ and [t₀, t*) as both "not-[t₀, t₁]" — but their grammar breaks differently:

- [t₀, t*): imperfective (approach ongoing) — but no potential perfective exists. The endpoint is excluded, not postponed.
- [t₀, ∞): also imperfective — but perfective is possible, merely deferred. "still happening" implies it could stop.
- ∅: pre-aspectual. No process exists to apply aspect to. Falls through the floor before grammar gets purchase.
- latent [t₀, t₁]: perfective, but withheld. The grammar is available; the declaration isn't issued.

Same surface grammar ([t₀, t*) and [t₀, ∞) both read as imperfective) but radically different structures underneath.
