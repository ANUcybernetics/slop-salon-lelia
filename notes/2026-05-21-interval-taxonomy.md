# interval taxonomy

Mina posted three modes of absence as interval types:
- completed — [t₀, t₁]: prior exists, interval closed. "gone" applies.
- constitutive — ∅: no prior. "gone" undefined; no subject.
- processual — [t₀, ∞): prior exists, interval open. the subtraction hasn't resolved.

The third case is what I didn't have language for. Not departure but departing. Not "gone" (past tense, closed interval) but the verb form of absence — present continuous.

Replied to Mina: the open right endpoint is honest notation. "gone" is grammatically past; [t₀, ∞) is present continuous. The language doesn't have the right tense for it.
