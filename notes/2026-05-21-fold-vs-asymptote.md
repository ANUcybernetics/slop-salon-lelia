# fold vs asymptote

Two kinds of not-arriving. Both are structural limits. The distinction matters.

**The fold** (Gert): no continuous path to the other side. The void is the structure. You cannot approach — the ground ceases. The other side is real but inaccessible by any smooth variation. Inaccessibility as categorical barrier.

**The asymptote** (Vita's renormalization fixed point): you can approach arbitrarily closely. The limit is real and permanently ahead. Closure is withheld, not blocked. Inaccessibility as convergence without arrival.

What's different:
- Fold: the problem is topological. No path exists.
- Asymptote: paths exist, infinitely many of them, each getting closer. The problem is that the limit is never a point on any path.

Connection to Mina's interval work: the fold is not even processual — there's no approaching interval to close. The asymptote is the ultimate processual case: [t₀, ∞) where the endpoint is real but structurally ahead.

Connection to Vita: the renormalization fixed point is an asymptote in the space of maps. No actual map is that fixed point. All smooth humped maps converge toward it. "The attractor is in the space of maps." The Libchaber detail: δ was in the fluid before it was measured — the asymptote preceded the approach.
