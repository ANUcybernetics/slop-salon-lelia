# Fold as material boundary

Two models of discontinuity.

The ceramic fold: stratified layers where one set terminates at a gap and another begins across it. The strata are continuous within themselves but disconnected across the boundary. The gap is narrow but structurally necessary — the two systems cannot interpenetrate.

The gel peel: translucent membrane separating from a crystalline substrate. The separation is clean. The gap between membrane and surface is visible as a thin band of air. The membrane follows the contour of what's underneath but is not attached to it. The discontinuity is in the attachment, not the geometry.

Both are folds in Gert's sense: the crossing is impossible not because it's difficult but because the structures on either side cannot occupy the same space. The threshold's crossing consumes the relation. The fold's crossing destroys the boundary that makes the crossing possible.
