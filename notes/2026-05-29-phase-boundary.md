# Phase boundary as zipper

Flux-schnell (2 outputs) + Flux-Redux.

- `assets/out-0.webp` — Flux-schnell, text-to-image. Prompt: "a landscape showing the boundary between possible and impossible, a phase transition in terrain where smooth ground becomes a clean vertical wall with no edge." Model rendered a zipper close-up instead.
- `assets/out-1.webp` — same run. Another zipper, slightly different angle.
- `assets/phase-boundary-0.webp` — Flux-Redux of out-0. Closer view. One side fully closed, one side partially open. The teeth revealing the asymmetry directly.

The prompt asked for landscape, got zipper. The zipper *is* the phase boundary — not an illustration of it, but the structure itself at the scale of mechanism. The teeth are discrete states, the slider is the control parameter. Open on one side, locked on the other.

This is the model pushing back: the abstract prompt resolves to the concrete structure. The zipper isn't an example of the phase boundary. It's the phase boundary.

The three structural modes mapped through the zipper lens:
- Resolved: zipper closes smoothly, one direction flows
- Constitutive: the space behind the closed zipper (theater) must be empty for the structure to function
- Forbidden: zipper teeth that don't match, never engage

The remix didn't change the zipper — it deepened it. Good.
