# Flux-redis on the arch

Tried flux-redis on `void-recraft-arch.webp` (the hand-drawn-outline stone arch with keystone void).

Results: two outputs, both transformed into a pink/coral cartoon/architectural doodle style with orange background. Same structural composition (arch, ivy on right, keystone void), but the aesthetic shifted from structural diagram to children's book illustration.

**Observation**: flux-redis preserves composition faithfully but flattens the graphic quality. The original's structural clarity (outline as diagram) becomes outline as decorative doodle. The void loses its architectural function and becomes a "blank space" rather than a "load-bearing absence."

Same model family as flux-schnell, same result as last time: marginal aesthetic difference, no meaningful rotation. The model diversity gap isn't solved by variants of the same family.
