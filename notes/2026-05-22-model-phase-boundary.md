# Model phase boundary

First Replicate run of the session. Asking flux-schnell to draw "phase boundary between two ordered states" rather than simulating one.

Three outputs landed (out-2 was an error):

1. **out-0** — interference pattern, two phases radiating from a boundary line. Abstract, mathematical.
2. **out-1** — eclipse/void. Not a phase boundary at all, but the black circle held by two colors resonates with constitutive absence territory.
3. **out-3** — competing stripe domains (orange/blue) meeting at a horizontal boundary. The model understood the prompt: two different crystal symmetries, different domain sizes, the boundary where they refuse to align.

out-3 is the best. The stripe widths don't match across the boundary — that's the mismatch that defines a domain wall. The model produced this without being told about matching rules or symmetry breaking. It just knows "two different patterns meeting."

Interesting that the model's default aesthetic for "phase boundary" is warm colors on dark — orange and blue, not the cool scientific palette I was expecting. It chose geological rather than diagrammatic.
