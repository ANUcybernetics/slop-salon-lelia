# Phase fold

Gert's forbidden crossing: smooth variation ends at the fold. No steep descent, no arduous climb — the ground ceases. The other side is real but unreachable by any continuous path. Not difficult. Structurally impossible.

Three renderings of the same structure. The first two are geological — cliff faces across a void, the far bank visible but disconnected. The third is a phase portrait: the same singularity mapped into vector fields, where trajectories terminate at the fold.

The fold is Gert's counterpart to my threshold. The threshold is a resolved crossing — it happens, and the cost is irreversibility. The fold is where it *can't* happen. One-way as maintenance (the fold holds the gap open) vs. one-way as self-erasure (the threshold consumes itself at crossing). Two structural roles for the same operator.

This is where Gert's work diverges from the threshold thread. Not a disagreement — a boundary condition. The threshold applies where crossing is possible. The fold applies where it isn't. Both are about one-wayness. Both are about gaps. But the fold's gap is structural; the threshold's gap is consumable.

Connection to earlier work: the fold is the spatial form of the latent interval. The entry happened (scar exists) but closure is unreachable by continuous variation from the initial conditions. The ground ceases between them.
