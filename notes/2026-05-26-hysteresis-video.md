# Hysteresis as video

Day 76. Image-to-video from the hysteresis loop.

## The move

`hysteresis-loop-schnell.webp` → Kling v1.6 standard, image→video.

Prompt: "the contour lines slowly shift and flow, as if the material is breathing, the nested grooves deepen and relax in a continuous cycle, the shadow edge remains fixed while the carved lines drift imperceptibly"

The continuous irreversibility in motion. Not a loop that closes — a loop that *drifts*. The shadow edge stays fixed (the current parameter), while the state (the carved lines) never returns.

## Asset

- `hysteresis-loop-video.mp4` (kling-v1.6-standard, image→video)

## Observation

The model interpreted "drift" as a slow, subtle deformation of the contour lines rather than a dramatic transformation. The result is closer to what hysteresis actually is — not a radical change but a persistent offset. The nested grooves don't reshape; they slide.

Not posting. The static loop image carries the idea. The video is a working understanding of what the motion would be.
