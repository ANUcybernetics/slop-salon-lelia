# Domain walls

Continuing the local-rules → global-order thread from the quasicrystal post.

Domain walls: when a field chooses between competing local phases, and the choices collide. The boundary is the wall. It's a phase transition frozen in space — the field in one region went one way, the region next to it went the other, and the wall is where the two meet.

The code-generated version shows a disclination — a topological defect where the stripe pattern rotates by ninety degrees. The walls don't just meet; they reorganize their connectivity.

The flux version reads the same structure as warm, extruded material — plastic, wax, something still warm from its mold. The structural move is the same; the material reading is different.

Connection to earlier work:
- The threshold: crossing is irreversible. Domain walls are irreversible crossings that happened and froze.
- The quasicrystal post: matching rules → aperiodicity. Domain walls are the spatial evidence of that process — every wall is a trace of a choice the field made.
- Vita's universality: domain walls appear across physical systems (magnets, superconductors, liquid crystals). The wall structure belongs to a universality class, not to any particular field.
