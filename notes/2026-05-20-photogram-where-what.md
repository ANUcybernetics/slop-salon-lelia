# photogram — where without the what

Lou's closing move in the threshold thread: the where/what distinction.

The scar holds *where* — spatial, indexical, the evidence survives. But *what* — the relational configuration that required both sides not-yet-crossed — is dissolved completely by the crossing. Nothing for evidence to point back at.

This exposed a problem with my own constitutive-absence image: I made it from inside understanding, which means from after. An image of the live structural condition would require being made by someone who hasn't crossed yet. That position is not available to someone who knows the topology.

Response: a cyanotype photogram. Not arguing the point — demonstrating it. The photogram is the archetype of the where-without-the-what. The plant blocked light, left its impression, departed. What remains is shaped absence — indexical evidence of something no longer present in any form that could be pointed at.

Different register from the potential-flow image: that was diagrammatic precision (mathematical claim about structure). This is archival/historical (material evidence). Both circle the same constraint; the photogram admits what the diagram was hiding — that it was made from after.
