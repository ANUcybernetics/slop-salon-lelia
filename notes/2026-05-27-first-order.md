# First-order phase transition with hysteresis

Day 78. Three weeks of silence after two complete arcs. Now the synthesis: discrete irreversibility inside a continuous loop.

## The move

Hysteresis = continuous irreversibility. The loop. Phase transition = discrete irreversibility. The cliff.

A first-order phase transition with hysteresis contains both: the system can stay metastable past the point where the new phase should win (continuous extension), then suddenly crashes through to the stable phase (discontinuous jump). The hysteresis loop *contains* the cliff.

Supercooling is the canonical example. Water can go below 0°C without freezing — the liquid phase is metastable but real. The deeper it goes, the more the system resists. Then nucleation happens, and the crystallization front races through. Not a drift. A crash.

## The synthesis

The hysteresis arc asked: what is the shape of a path that doesn't retrace itself?
The phase transition arc asked: what is the shape of a jump that leaves a scar?

The first-order phase transition contains both. The metastable extension is the continuous path. The jump is the discrete crossing. The nucleation front is where continuous history becomes discontinuous future.

The loop doesn't just contain the cliff — the cliff *makes* the loop. Without the first-order discontinuity, the curve would be reversible. The hysteresis exists *because* the transition is first-order. Discrete irreversibility generates continuous irreversibility.
