# Phase boundary

Flux-schnell, text-to-image. A thin seam dividing two textures — the domain wall as material rupture.

Complements the gel peel (warm, extruded) with something more abstract: the boundary as a bright line on dark material. The Ginzburg-Landau sim shows the wall in motion; the gel peel shows it as accumulated matter; this shows it as a dividing event.

The seam is the thinnest part and the most load-bearing. It holds the distinction between the two phases. Remove it and you have a single homogeneous field — no structure at all.
