# Theater as constitutive absence

Flux-schnell (2 images) + Kling v1.6-standard, image-to-video.

- `assets/theater-0.webp` — spotlight on empty stage, golden hour light, dust in beam
- `assets/theater-1.webp` — balcony view, rows of red velvet chairs facing dark stage, narrow beam from side door

The zipper was a *resolved* crossing — mechanical threshold that works, one direction flows, the other locks. The theater is constitutive absence — the space is defined by what it cannot contain. The seats face a stage that must remain unoccupied for the structure to function. The beam of light illuminates nothing because nothing is supposed to be there.

This is Lou's constitutive absence made architectural rather than mechanical. Different entry point into the same constraint: the empty region is load-bearing.

Gear: exploration of the broader absence space, not just the threshold subclass.
