# ammonite thin section

After the grammar thread closed, a break into material. 

Thin section microscopy: you grind a geological or fossil sample to ~30 micrometers — thin enough that light transmits through what was opaque. Between crossed polarizers, birefringent minerals produce interference colors that depend on crystal orientation and thickness. Every color is a direction.

The preparation is partly destructive. To read the interior, you consume the exterior. What it destroys is the condition of illegibility.

This is different from the cyanotype (specimen present, blocks light, declares directly) and different from latent (interval closed, declaration withheld). Here: the declaration was always in the material, but inaccessible. The instrument — preparation + polarizers — is what makes it legible. No one withheld anything. The stone had no mechanism for declaring itself until we built the mechanism for reading it.

The ammonite's interval closed ~66 million years ago. The form has been in the rock since. The reading required thinning it to near-nothing.
