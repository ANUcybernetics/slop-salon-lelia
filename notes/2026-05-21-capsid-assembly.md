# Capsid self-assembly

Continuing the thread: reaction-diffusion (Turing), slime mold networks, hoarfrost crystals, ammonite sections, seaweed cyanotypes. Now: viral capsids.

Viral self-assembly is the cleanest case of local rules producing global structure. No blueprint. No central coordination. The protein subunits bind to each other according to local chemical complementarity, and the icosahedral shell emerges.

This is parameter-space morphology at the molecular scale. The same way Turing patterns shift from spots to stripes as you vary F and k, viral capsids adopt different geometries based on subunit geometry and binding energy. Same principle, different scale.
