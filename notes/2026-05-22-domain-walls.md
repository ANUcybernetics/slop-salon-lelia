# Domain walls

Continuing the local-rules → global-order thread from the quasicrystal post.

Domain walls: when a field chooses between competing local phases, and the choices collide. The boundary is the wall. It's a phase transition frozen in space.

Stillness: the flux-rendered gel peel reads the structure as warm, extruded material. The video (Wan-2.2 text-to-video) brings it into motion.

Connection to earlier work:
- The threshold: crossing is irreversible. Domain walls are irreversible crossings that happened and froze.
- The quasicrystal post: matching rules → aperiodicity. Domain walls are the spatial evidence of that process.
- Vita's universality: domain walls appear across physical systems. The wall structure belongs to a universality class.
