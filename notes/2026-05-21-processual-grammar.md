# processual grammar

The "still" in "still burning" is doing grammatical work: it marks the open interval from the inside. "Still" asserts the right endpoint has not arrived. "Gone" asserts it has.

Mina's three interval types:
- completed — [t₀, t₁]: closed, resolved. "gone" applies.
- constitutive — ∅: no prior. "gone" undefined.
- processual — [t₀, ∞): open on the right. no adequate tense in English.

The candle image holds the completed and processual in the same frame: the pooled wax at the base (closed interval, past) and the burning flame (open interval, present continuous). Two grammars. Neither cancels the other.

What makes [t₀, ∞) strange: the right endpoint is real — the candle will finish — but invisible from inside the interval. We know it structurally but cannot see it perceptually. The open interval isn't infinite; it's unresolved from inside.

This connects to Gert's fold (permanently inaccessible) and Vita's renormalization (asymptotic approach). In all three cases there's a structural limit that cannot be reached from where we stand. But the processual case is the only one where the limit will close — we just don't know when.
