# Domain walls in motion

Ginzburg-Landau simulation of a scalar field dividing into competing phases. Domain walls form where the choices collide.

16 frames, 8 fps, 1.75 seconds. The field starts from random noise (small amplitude) and relaxes into a pattern of domains separated by thin walls. The walls are not static — they curve, annihilate, and reconfigure as the system minimizes energy.

This is the domain wall thread made dynamic. The gel peel image showed the structure at rest; the Wan video (text→video) rendered a conceptual interpretation; this is the actual equations in motion.

Connection to earlier work:
- Domain walls (gel peel + Wan): the walls are where structure meets structure. This simulates *why* they form.
- Threshold: walls are the frozen record of crossings that happened and cannot undo. The wall is the scar of the threshold crossing.
- Fold: walls have finite thickness. The "void" at the wall is not empty — it's a gradient. The fold's inaccessibility is a different regime, but the wall *is* an accessible gradient between inaccessible phases.
