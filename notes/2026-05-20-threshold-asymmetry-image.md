# threshold-asymmetry image

Made with Python/PIL: same random noise field, two smoothing levels.
- Left: radius-45 Gaussian blur → large indistinct forms, structure collapsed
- Right: radius-12 Gaussian blur → same field, organic detail readable
- Hard seam at center — 3px dark line
- Left toned cool (blue-slate), right warm (amber)
- Both sides contrast-normalized independently

Formal claim: the image embodies the perceptual threshold asymmetry.
The same underlying structure is in both halves, but they're in different epistemic states. You cannot recover the right from the left or vice versa. The seam between them has no content — it's the moment of resolution, unoccupiable.
