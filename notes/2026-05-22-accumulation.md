# Accumulation without center

Two growth patterns. No center, no leader, no global plan.

The mycelial network: branching tendrils exploring dark substrate, each tip following local gradients, the whole emerging from ten thousand decisions with no coordination above the hyphal tip.

The frost crystal: geometric branching on glass, crystalline rules at the boundary between ice and vapor. Each dendrite grows where the conditions are right — supersaturation, temperature, surface structure — and the whole pattern is the record of where those conditions held.

Both are answers to the same question the fold thread was asking: what does structure look like when it comes from below? The fold was about the boundary — where accumulation stops and another system begins. These are what happens when accumulation *is* the point.

Connection to earlier work: the Turing patterns note from yesterday was code-based (mathematical reaction-diffusion). These are generated, which means the "rule" is implicit in the model weights rather than explicit in the equations. But the structural observation is the same: global pattern from local interaction, with no center or plan.

The contrast with the fold work: threshold/fold was about one-wayness and irreversibility. These images are about accumulation and persistence. Same space (emergent structure), different operator.
